(Book 6 тАУ Bhishma Parva). It is a conversation between Lord Krishna and Prince

	тАв	Arjuna, a warrior prince, is confused and reluctant to fight against his own relatives, teachers, and friends.
	тАв	He expresses his grief and refuses to engage in battle.
	тАв	Krishna, his charioteer and divine guide, advises him about his duty (Dharma) as a Kshatriya (warrior).

	тАв	Krishna explains different paths to liberation (Moksha):
	тАв	Karma Yoga (Path of Selfless Action) тАУ Perform duty without attachment to results.

	тАв	Bhakti Yoga (Path of Devotion) тАУ Complete surrender and devotion to God.

	тАв	Krishna reveals his divine nature and universal form (Vishvarupa), displaying his supreme power.
	тАв	Arjuna is overwhelmed and surrenders to Krishna as the Supreme Being.

	тАв	Krishna emphasizes that one should act without attachment, surrender to God, and maintain equanimity in success and failure.
	тАв	The Gita concludes with Krishna encouraging Arjuna to rise and fulfill his duty with faith and devotion.

	тАв	Perform your duty without attachment to results (Nishkama Karma).
	тАв	The soul is eternal and beyond birth and death (Atman is immortal).
	тАв	Detachment from material desires leads to inner peace and liberation.
	тАв	True devotion and surrender to God bring spiritual fulfillment.
	тАв	Balance in life through wisdom, self-discipline, and righteousness is the key to success.
